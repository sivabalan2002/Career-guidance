Details about Top Courses after 12th for Commerce Students

BCom

Bachelor of Commerce, also known as BCom/BCom (Hons) is an undergraduate degree that can be pursued by students
who have cleared Class 12th in Science, Arts or Commerce stream. However, preference is given to students who
have studied Commerce at 10+2 level. The duration of the course spans over a period of three years in Indian
colleges/Universities.BCom admission is based on marks obtained in Class 12th, however some colleges may have
an entrance exam for admissions.

Top Courses after 12th Commerce with Mathematics

BCom (Honours)

BCom (Honours) is one of the most popular undergraduate courses among students belonging to Commerce with
Mathematics stream. This course is also one of the most widely offered BCom courses at university level. SRCC,
LSR, Hindu College, Loyola College and St Xavier’s Kolkata are some of the top ranked colleges in India for
B Com (Honours).

Also readTop BCOMI(honours) colleges

BCom Accounting and Taxation

Students who wish to make a career in Banking and Insurance should take up BCom Accounting and Taxation. The
course curriculum includes Indian Tax System, Financial Accounting, Value Added Tax and Central Tax Procedure,
Financial Accounting, Principles of Management and Business Communication.

BCom Statistics

BCom Statistics opens up various avenues in Economic Research and Analysis. Civil Services, Data Analytics,
Education, Consulting and Government Organisations are some of the popular career options in this field. The
course includes study of Applied Information Economics, Applied Statistics, Biostatistics, Business Statistics,
Data Analysis, Demography, etc.

Also readTop statistics colleges

BCom in Management Accounting & International Finance

BCom in Management Accounting & International Finance is a good option to choose at graduation level if one
wishes to pursue International Business at Masters level. The course provides exposure to international business
and US CMA (Certified Management Accountant).

BCom in Accounting

Those who wish to go for competitive exams and banking exams or take up Chartered Accountancy or Company
Secretaryship, must opt for BCom in Accounting. The course includes Indian Financial System and Financial Market
Operation, Financial Management, Cost & Management Accounting, Business Mathematics & Statistics, Economics,
Principles of Marketing, Financial Accounting and Business Regulatory Framework.

Also readTop accounting colleges

BCom Applied Economics

This branch of BCom trains the students in theoretical knowledge of various aspects of Applied Economics and
how they can be used in the economic environment to critically analyse problems and solutions. Course curriculum
includes Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, Public Economics, Indian Economic Development, etc.

BCom Banking & Finance

BCom Banking & Finance course covers all the aspects of BFSI industry such as Banking, Banking Law, Insurance
Law, Insurance Risk & Insurance Regulations, Accounting, etc. After a degree in BCom Banking & Finance one can
opt for careers such as Auditor, Analyst, Equity Manager, Wealth Manager, Account Manager, Stockbroker, Market
Analyst, etc.

BCom (General) course is one of the most popular options after Class 12th for Commerce without Maths students.

BCom Business Administration

BCom Marketing

This specialization of BCom trains the student in techniques and methods of planning and managing marketing
activities for a business organization. Career opportunities after BCom Marketing are many, as Marketing is

BCom Tourism & Travel Management

BCom Tourism & Travel Management is specific to Tourism industry. This course trains students in theoretical
and practical aspects of the industry. Apart from business operation and other core commerce subjects, the
students are taught financial relevance of tourism, impact on world economy, environmental laws and regulations,
customer-care and servicing, international travel laws and tourism management.
